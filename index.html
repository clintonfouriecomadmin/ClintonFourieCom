Blog post 01; DevOps discussion (28/07/2020)

DevOps pipelines are an investment and testing different solutions for different languages will become "DevOps Best Practise".
There are many solutions out there to choose from, some of these don't understand the fundamentals of networking principles.
Do you understand your OSI model, communication protocols or principles, App security, pipeline security, DNS leaks, Data leaks?
If not, then it will be much safer for you to keep everything under one umbrella, rather than trying to build out your pipeline
over secure or insecure connections. Just imagine someone intercepts your code during the commit phase or injects code into
your pipeline, whos going to tell the clients that every app is a potential hidden botnet. It is one thing, thinking your code
needs to be tried and tested, as your pipeline should also be monitored, patched and upgraded for any potential
vulnrabilities.
So you need your trusted and tested pipeline and you also need a "development pipeline" the one you can play around with.
